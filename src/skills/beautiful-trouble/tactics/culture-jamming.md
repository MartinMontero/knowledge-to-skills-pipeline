# Culture Jamming / Détournement

> "We are not anti-consumer, we are pro-creation. We want to give people the tools to become producers, not just consumers." — Culture Jamming tradition

## Overview

Culture jamming is the practice of subverting cultural symbols, corporate messaging, and dominant narratives to reveal hidden power structures and propose alternative meanings. Originally developed by the Dadaists and later formalized by the Ad Busters movement, culture jamming turns the tools of mass media against themselves.

## When to Use

**Best for:**
- Responding to corporate greenwashing or deceptive advertising
- Creating media moments that go viral with transformative messaging
- Engaging younger demographics through irony and humor
- Building alternative narratives without authorities censoring your message
- Creating memorable visual symbols that reframe political debates

**Strategic Considerations:**
- Humor can make serious messages more accessible but may undermine gravitas
- Corporate targets may respond with legal threats (often bluffs)
- Works best when the jam is clearly connected to a real campaign demand
- Consider: Is the target powerful enough to respond? Will we reach new audiences?

## Core Techniques

### 1. Billboard Liberation
Modify existing advertising to reveal hidden messages or satirize the original.

**Steps:**
1. Scout locations for high-traffic billboards
2. Design modifications that work with existing imagery
3. Consider legal risks and photographer-friendly timing
4. Document thoroughly for media distribution

### 2. Brand Hijacking
Use a company's own branding against them to highlight contradictions.

**Examples:**
- Using McDonald's golden arches to protest climate impact
- Reclaiming Nike's "Just Do It" for labor rights
- Subverting Apple marketing to critique electronics waste

### 3. Meme Warfare
Create viral digital content that parodies or subverts dominant narratives.

**Platform considerations:**
- Meet audiences where they are (often TikTok, Instagram, X)
- Balance irony with clarity about the underlying message
- Prepare for rapid iteration based on what goes viral

### 4. Guerilla Theater
Performances in public spaces that interrupt normal activity and draw attention to issues.

**Planning checklist:**
- [ ] Script reviewed for clarity and impact
- [ ] Location scouted and permits checked (if required)
- [ ] Media strategy prepared (photographers, press release)
- [ ] Safety protocols for participants
- [ ] Exit strategy if confronted

## Case Study: #OccupyWallStreet

The Occupy movement used culture jamming effectively:
- "We are the 99%" reframed economic inequality
- Occupation of public space made abstract inequality tangible
- Meme culture amplified messages organically

**What worked:** Decentralized messaging allowed local adaptation
**What didn't:** Lack of clear demands limited narrative coherence

## Safety & Legality

| Concern | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|
| Legal consequences | Research local laws; many jurisdictions allow artistic expression |
| Corporate retaliation | Document everything; public attention often deters lawsuits |
| Co-optation | Maintain clear connection to concrete demands |
